Visitor Information

Location 📌


Buckingham Palace is located in Central London making it close to other tourist attractions. Attractions such as Big Ben, London Eye, St. James Park, and many others are just minutes away by walk. If you're not a fan or walking? Buckingham Palace and the nearby attractions are accessible by public transport like the tube or bus, the true London experience!


It is best for visitors to arrive early to get a good view of the Changing of the Guard, and allow extra time for travel and security checks!


Getting There 🚇


Street Way Signs (Photo by artellliii72 on Pixabay)

The Palace is connected by public transport, and nearby many tube stations.

  • Tube: Victoria, Green Park, St. James's Park
  • Bus: There are Multiple Stop situated around Buckingham Palace
  • Walking: It is located near Westminster and other attractions. There are signs nearby to direct you where places are.


Opening Times 🕰


Visitors are allowed to visit the exterior of Buckingham Palace all year around, 24 hours everyday. However, the State Rooms are only open to the public from early July to late September. It is strict with its timing and requires for early booking and ticket reservations. Though, tours such as the Royal Mews or the King's Gallery are open outside of the summer season, subject to availability.


Tickets 🎟


Tickets are needed to visit areas such as the State Rooms, Royal Mews, or the King's Gallery. Prices vary based on age and ticket type such as adult or child. It is recommended to book in advance especially duing peak tourist seasons.


If you're looking to book tickets for the State Rooms, visit visitlondon.com!


Accessibility ♿


Buckingham Palace provides support for visitors who may have needs, such as wheelchairs access or lift access. Accessibile toilets and facilites are also available during opening times. Please contact directly for more information.
